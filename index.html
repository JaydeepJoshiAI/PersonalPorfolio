Publication

Impact of Feature Evolution and Selection on Phishing Detection using Machine Learning
IEEE ICDM Workshop on MLC 2022 : Oct 8, 2022
  • The cybersecurity domain is challenging to consistently and effectively identify a wide range of phishing attempts due to the high rate of change in the phishing detection area.
  • In this study, we investigate how features' impacts on phishing detection change with time.
  • In multiple phishing datasets, we found the important features that might offer comparative findings. Three well-known datasets from 2012, 2015, and 2020 were compared and contrasted based on the similarities and differences in their feature sets.
  • With an average of 50% less features, our studies produced classification results equivalent to those of the prior study. As a result, machine learning models' detection performance increased significantly without sacrificing accuracy.
  • We also achieved reasonable accuracy using ensemble feature selection techniques, even though using on average 80% less features.
